4.18 personal statement 段落试水

Briefly describe a personal goal you have set for yourself, and how long you have been working to achieve this goal. Who did you turn to for advice or help, and what was their role? what did you learn about yourself? (maximum 350 words)

Because Business Management or Economics will be my first choice for my university course application, the relevant grades in my Economics course are very important. I have set myself a goal for the final IGCSE exam of Economics, which is to achieve an A*. Although I had not been exposed to Economics before high school, I soon find that it is closely related to my ideal profession and I am strongly interested in it.

I put my effort in Economics as much as possible since I entered Grade 10. There are plenty of basic principles and academic terms that I am unfamiliar with, reducing my efficiency of understanding the textbook. As a result, I turned to some books for help, which illustrated the key terms in both micro and macro economics very precisely and offered me a quick access to fill my knowledge gap.

In addition, I have asked my economics teacher as well as my classmates who are good at Economics for help. They act as my mistake correctors and instructors. As some specific formats and exam strategies are tested in Economics exams, it is essential for me to ask my teacher to help me correct the nonstandard formats I have written. Therefore, my accuracy in tests has been significantly improved. Besides, learning from peers is certainly a good method. My classmates recommended some of their tips for studying Economics to me, which I found very useful and have added to my skill set.

During the process of pursuing an A* of Economics, I learnt that the two words-“precise” and “modest” are the keys to achieve my personal goal. A keen sense of judgement to economic cases is highly required, and that is what I should learn from others continuously.
